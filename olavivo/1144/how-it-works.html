Automated trading isn't new — institutional desks have used algorithms for decades. What has changed in recent years is access. AI-driven platforms now let retail investors interact with the same general approach: software that scans markets, applies rules, and executes orders without manual input.

Quantum AI is one such platform, focused on cryptocurrency markets. This article is an informational overview of how that category of platform generally works.

What an AI Trading Platform Actually Does

At a basic level, an AI trading platform performs three tasks continuously: ingest market data, evaluate it against a trained model, and decide whether to open or close a position. The "AI" element refers to the model — typically a machine-learning system trained on historical price movement, volume, and related signals.

Once a user funds an account and activates automated mode, the software handles the routine of monitoring markets and placing orders. The user's role shifts from active trader to account holder, reviewing performance and adjusting settings if the platform permits.

Why Crypto?

Cryptocurrency markets operate 24 hours a day, seven days a week, with no scheduled closing bell. That continuous activity suits automated systems: there is always price action to analyse, and a software-driven approach does not require a human to be awake. This is part of why platforms like Quantum AI focus on crypto rather than traditional equities.
